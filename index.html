About

30+ Years at the
Edge of Computing

Clay Fandre has spent his career solving problems most computers can't. As a Senior HPC Engineer, he designs, deploys, and optimizes the high-performance computing infrastructure that powers breakthroughs in aerospace, defense, science, and enterprise — the clusters and supercomputing systems where ordinary hardware simply doesn't apply.

With a foundation in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Minnesota Duluth and over three decades in the field, Clay has built deep expertise across the full HPC lifecycle: from architecture and procurement through systems administration, performance tuning, automation, and multi-vendor consulting.

Currently at RedLine Performance Solutions — one of the most respected names in the HPC industry — Clay brings the same competitive precision to engineering that he once brought to the basketball court at UMD.

Clay is currently on contract at NASA's Ames Research Center in Silicon Valley, supporting the NASA Advanced Supercomputing (NAS) Division — the agency's premier supercomputing facility, where the machines that model spacecraft, simulate climate, and design the next era of flight live.

🖥️

The Hyperwall

One of the largest and most powerful visualization systems in the world — a 300-square-foot wall of screens pushing over one billion pixels. Now in its fourth generation, completely rebuilt in 2024, it lets scientists walk up to their simulations and explore supercomputer-scale results in real time.

🚀

Athena

NASA's most powerful supercomputer, brought online in early 2026 — 20.13 petaflops of peak performance, housed in the Modular Supercomputing Facility at Ames.

🛰️

Aitken & Friends

Petascale systems like Aitken run around the clock in a modular facility built for growth — the workhorses behind NASA's aeronautics, Artemis, and Earth-science workloads.

🌎

The Mission

From simulating launch aerodynamics to rendering global climate models, NAS computing touches nearly every NASA mission. Keeping infrastructure like that healthy is what an HPC engineer lives for.
